Browser-based Locally Hosted Arabic LLM Optimization

Shahad Nassar, Fatima Falah, Hoor Muneer

University of Bahrain

Abstract

This work evaluates the effectiveness of several model compression and optimization techniques on large language models, namely quantization, pruning, and knowledge distillation, with a focus on Arabic natural language performance and browser deployment. The primary methods investigated are X for quantization, SparseGPT and Wanda for pruning, and X for knowledge distillation. The paper concludes with a comprehensive comparative analysis between the tested compression methods, comparing their compression and accuracy tradeoff and discussing their practical effectiveness for limited-resource deployment.
